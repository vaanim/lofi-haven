About Our Class

IN4MATX 172 / PUBHLTH 106 - Project in Health Informatics is a hands-on, project-based course that allows us to apply health informatics concepts in real-world scenarios. As students in this course, we will bridge the gap between theory and practice by working on a quarter-long project, either self-initiated or guided by an external client. This course serves as the second in the Health/Medical Informatics series and acts as the capstone for the Undergraduate Minor in Health Informatics.

Throughout the quarter, we will explore current health IT systems, mobile health applications, and healthcare data analysis. Our projects will focus on identifying gaps in healthcare practices and designing innovative technological solutions to address them. Using Contextual Design principles, we will develop skills in conducting interviews, observations, and surveys to assess user needs and evaluate technologies.

What We Will Learn:

  • Gain a deeper understanding of health and healthcare practices.
  • Design and develop innovative health informatics solutions.
  • Engage with healthcare organizations, faculty, and research teams.

This class is different from traditional lecture-based courses. Instead of just listening to lectures, we will learn through hands-on experiences, collaborative projects, and iterative development. To succeed, we will need to be proactive, organized, and adaptable.
